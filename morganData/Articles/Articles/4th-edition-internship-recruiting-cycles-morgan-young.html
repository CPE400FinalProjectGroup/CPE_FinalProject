Recruiting Timelines for the Top 5 Most Lucrative Internships/Industries

[this one's gonna be a short one]

1️⃣ Big Tech

Prestigious internships like Google STEP & Meta University begin recruitment around August. The same can be said for top Product Management programs (APM). Applications typically close from late August to early September, interview rounds are conducted from September to October, and final offers are sent out around November/December.

2️⃣ Finance

Finance (investment banking + similar) internships, specifically summer analyst roles at bulge bracket banks, have one of the earliest recruiting cycles. Summer internship recruiting begins the summer of the year prior. So if you're going for a Summer 2024 IB internship, the recruiting cycle has already begun.

3️⃣ Consulting

Similar to Finance, consulting firms begin internship recruitment cycles very early. To be specific, MBB (the royal trifecta of management consulting) typically opens in early to mid-June. Other consulting firms (i.e. PwC, Deloitte, KPMG) keep their program applications open a little bit and into the fall semester. For technology consulting programs, I've seen these applications stay open even through the spring. But it's better to start early!

4️⃣ Entertainment & Media

Depending on the role you're going for, entertainment companies typically recruit throughout the entire academic year. At Disney, some internship roles and programs open as early as August and some summer interns get offers as late as early June.

5️⃣ NPOs, NGOs & Government

Specific fellowships and structured programs for this sector will have a similar recruiting timeline to tech, where applications open in late summer/early fall. Other roles that are "stand-alone" or not part of a specific program, are generally released on a rolling basis throughout the academic year.
