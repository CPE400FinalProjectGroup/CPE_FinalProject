During my first season with Disney, I was fortunate enough to have been offered a fall term extension, halfway through my summer internship. Luckily, I had prepared for this conversation. I knew I wanted to limit myself to 20 hours per week and I needed to be able to flex my work schedule to accommodate my class schedule. My manager was very flexible and helped me "make it work."

The Play-by-Play on Getting Internship Extensions & Return Offers


1Ô∏è‚É£ Ask.

No, seriously. Too many people I know didn't get extensions or return offers simply because they didn't ask or didn't express interest in such opportunities. It's kind of like dating; try to make the 1st move. Real-life example: Last year, I talked with a fellow intern, and they said they wanted to extend or come back next year. I happened to know that his manager really liked him, and he even asked me how he could provide an exceptional internship experience for my fellow intern. So I returned to my friend and had this conversation: "Well, have you asked him [manager]?" "No." "Go back and ask him and see what he says." Not only was he able to secure a return offer, but was able to negotiate the timing so it fit his school and ROTC training schedule.


2Ô∏è‚É£ Keep that brag book!

At this point, I probably sound like a broken record, but I cannot emphasize enough how important it is to keep a "brag book." What is a brag book? It's simply a journal/diary of your accomplishments, achievements, and recognitions at work. It helps if these are specific to a project and quantify the level of impact that you had on the project. This could be how many people you worked with, increased performance metrics, or financial impact (increased revenue, decreased cost, etc.). Try to write 2-3 of these down weekly or biweekly. Once it's time to have that conversation, you'll be armed and ready with your brag book! üòé


3Ô∏è‚É£ Plan the logistics of the extension or return offer.

When I received my first extension offer, I had already planned how I would work it into my fall class schedule. I was very tactical in choosing my classes. The way it turned out, I went to school on Monday and Wednesday and for one hour on Friday. My intern contract was updated to reflect a "part-time" schedule, so I was to work 20 hours per week. I worked 8-8-4, Tuesday-Thursday-Friday. It worked perfectly with my class schedule, and I mimicked this strategy when I received my second extension (2022). Of course, going part-time isn't always an option with extensions, so you may have to consider taking a semester off or taking a "co-op" semester.
